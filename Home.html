Our Story

Murray and Elayna technically first met at (Best Man) Bao-Hoa's 23rd birthday on St. Patrick’s day in 2011—but Murray barely remembers that 😜

Their story didn’t take off until a few years later at (Maid of Honor) Laura's house in 2014; Laura (re-)introduced Elayna to her friend Murray, who was cute and adventurous and sometimes spoke in a Scottish accent when he drank… He was greeted by an energetic go-getter whose empathy for others was rivaled only by her ability to scatter tea mugs around the house. Over a summer filled with many hours-long conversations, hanging out at events like Brynn and Nick's engagement party, and a couple memorable camping trips with friends, they found themselves spending less and less time apart.

Knowing Elayna’s month-long departure to dance in the Edinburgh Tattoo would buck that trend, they went on a date to Edworthy Park to hash out what to make of their relationship. Murray was worried that they “weren’t compatible”, but luckily Elayna knew better—with more time together, Murray would see the forest past the trees. Fortunately, she was completely right: they picked up right where they left off when she returned—and have been inseparable ever since.

Elayna fell in love with the unique way that Murray saw the world, his constant desire to learn and grow, and the thoughtfulness he shared with her towards his friends. Murray was taken in by her undeniable talent at teaching, communicating with, and inspiring her students, along with the boundless diversity of new perspectives she shared and/or challenged him with. Different on the surface in so many ways, they seemed to fill each other’s gaps and make for a better whole.

And their love only deepened its roots through the next couple of years: a friends-trip to Mexico, the unforgettable “Yerp 2016” trip (to Switzerland, Germany, Austria, Croatia, and Venice), music festivals, “I love you”s, buying a house together, and then, while touring Scotland in August of 2018:

The Proposal

In the Isle of Skye, on top of a hill, at a picturesque and magical place called Fairy Glen, Murray got down on one knee, recounted a little speech he’d prepared about their journey together so far, and asked to continue adventuring with Elayna forever. Already crying and laughing and shaking with excitement, Elayna enthusiastically replied “Yes!”

They spent the rest of the day fawning over other Skye attractions (and of course each other), after which they enjoyed a romantic and mouth-wateringly delicious dinner at one of the island’s finest restaurants. Finally, thanks to prior help from Murray’s sister Kirsty and aunt Mairi, along with a wonderfully cooperative AirBnB host, upon arriving home from dinner that night, Elayna returned to a candlelit room, trails of rose petals, and ice-chilled champagne for two. The sight of pure surprise on her face was too much to bear, and for the first time, both of them were crying with joy together.
